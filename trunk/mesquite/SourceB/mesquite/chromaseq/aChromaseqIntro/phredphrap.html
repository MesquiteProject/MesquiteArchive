Phred/Phrap

Chromaseq will, using Phred and Phrap, convert a directory of chromatograms into an organized collection of files, including consensus sequences produced by Phred and Phrap, as modified by Mesquite's Chromaseq. Vital to this are the programs Phred and Phrap.

Phred (Green and Ewing, 2002) is a program that examines chromatogram files, and makes base calls therefrom. It also judges the quality of the base calls, and assigns a score to each called base.

Phrap ("Phil's Revised Assembly Program"; Green, 1999) is a program that takes the output of Phred, and forms contigs of the reads, producing in the process and ACE file.

These programs were originally written for large-scale sequencing efforts such as the Human Genome Project. Their source code is currently (2005) available free for most academic uses; compiled versions are available for a cost.

Details about these two programs, including how to obtain their source code, is available on the Phred and Phrap web site. See the Installing Phred & Phrap page in this manual for hints on compiling and installing Phred & Phrap.

References

Green, P. 1999. Phrap. http://phrap.org.

Green, P. and Ewing, B. 2002. Phred. http://phrap.org
